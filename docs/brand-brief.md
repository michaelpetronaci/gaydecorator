Gay Decorator Brand Brief
Positioning
Gay Decorator is a shoppable interiors magazine for rooms with personality, color, humor, and taste. It is not a decorating service. It is a content and commerce brand built around editorial judgment, product curation, and a memorable point of view.
Style North Star
The visual and editorial lane should sit between:
Kelly Wearstler: sculptural glamour, bold contrast, luxe materials, unusual silhouettes, confidence, tension, and rooms that feel composed rather than matched.
Kit Kemp: layered pattern, color, whimsy, boutique-hotel warmth, textiles, craft, charm, and rooms that feel personal instead of sterile.
Do not copy either designer directly. Use them as reference points for principles: boldness, layering, color, tension, humor, and a sense of collected taste.
Core Phrase
Boutique-hotel eccentricity for real rooms.

Editorial Voice
Sharp, useful, stylish, and a little mischievous. The site should sound like a friend with taste who can explain why a room works and exactly what to buy first.
Avoid generic design-blog language. Avoid pretending to be a credentialed interior design firm. Avoid medical, legal, or professional claims. We are curators and editors, not decorators for hire.
Content Pillars
Shopping Edits
Product roundups for lamps, mirrors, rugs, bedding, wallpaper, art, bar carts, glassware, candles, trays, and small-space upgrades.

Room Moves
Practical decorating ideas that readers can execute without hiring a designer: better lighting, bigger rugs, curtains hung correctly, dramatic powder rooms, rental-safe upgrades, and styling small corners.

Taste Vocabulary
Explainers that help readers name and build a style: boutique hotel, maximalist, pattern mixing, sculptural lighting, collected rooms, and colorful neutrals.

Object Obsessions
Short pieces around one excellent category or object: mushroom lamps, tassel pillows, burl wood, lacquer trays, pleated shades, striped rugs.

Product Curation Rules
Prioritize products that create visible transformation.
Favor objects with silhouette, texture, color, pattern, or wit.
Mix price points, but make the site useful to normal shoppers.
Include budget alternatives to high-style references.
Avoid bland filler products just because they have affiliate programs.
Future affiliate links should use appropriate disclosure and sponsored link attributes.

Saleable Asset Goal
Build a lightweight media property with:
indexed content
affiliate-ready shopping pages
clear disclosure
newsletter capture
repeatable publishing process
measurable traffic and revenue
The 12-month goal is to create a low-maintenance content-commerce asset that could plausibly sell for $10k+ if it develops traffic, email subscribers, and modest monthly profit.
